Sizing (horizontal)

Make an element as wide or as tall (relative to its parent) with our width and height utilities

Add w-25, w-50, w-75, w-100, or w-auto to set the vertical size of an element (relative to its parent).

<div class="w-25 p-3">Width 25%</div>
<div class="w-50 p-3">Width 50%</div>
<div class="w-75 p-3">Width 75%</div>
<div class="w-100 p-3">Width 100%</div>
<div class="w-auto p-3">Width auto</div>

Sizing (vertical)

Make an element as wide or as tall (relative to its parent) with our width and height utilities

Use the same classes above with d-inline-block to size vertically (relative to the parent).

This example creates and container with a set height.

<div style="height: 100px; background-color: rgba(255,255,100,0.5);">
  <div class="h-25 d-inline-block bg-light text-dark" style="width: 120px;">Height 25%</div>
  <div class="h-50 d-inline-block bg-light text-dark" style="width: 120px;">Height 50%</div>
  <div class="h-75 d-inline-block bg-light text-dark" style="width: 120px;">Height 75%</div>
  <div class="h-100 d-inline-block bg-light text-dark" style="width: 120px;">Height 100%</div>
  <div class="h-auto d-inline-block bg-light text-dark" style="width: 120px;">Height auto</div>
  </div>
